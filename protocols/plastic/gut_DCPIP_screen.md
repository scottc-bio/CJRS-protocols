## Abstract
Protocol for small-scale screening the BSFL gut community for oxidative plastic degradation.

---

## Materials & Equipment

**Solutions**
- [Bacterial LCFM](https://www.protocols.io/view/bacterial-liquid-carbon-free-media-8epv5kmrdv1b/v1)
- 1 mM DCPIP solution
- Sterile PBS

**Plastic**
- 50 mL Falcon tubes
- [Ethanol-washed PE, PP, PS](https://github.com/scottc-bio/CJRS-protocols/blob/main/protocols/plastic/plastic_washing.md)
- Spectrophotometric cuvettes
- 1.5 mL eppendorf tubes
- Plastic eppendorf pestle

**Equipment**
- P1000 + sterile tips
- P100 + sterile tips
- Shaking incubator
- Spectrophotometer
- Falcon tube centrifuge
- Eppendorf centrifuge

**Other**
- 10 final instar STD-reared larvae

## Method

**Preparing experimental cultures**

1. Add the following to 4 x sterile 50 mL falcon tubes:

 a. Nothing

 b. 100 mg washed PE
 
 c. 100 mg washed PP
 
 d. 100 mg washed PS

3. Add 10 mL bacterial LCFM to each tube

4. Prepare these tubes in duplicate for an uninoculated control

**Preparing larval midgut suspensions**

1. Take 10 STD-reared final instar larvae from a larger rearing

2. Dissect larvae and harvest midgut, transferring midguts to a single eppendorf tube

3. Add 1 mL sterile PBS and homogenise the midguts to a suspension using a plastic eppendorf pestle

**Inoculation of DCPIP gut screening cultures**

1. Add 100 μL of midgut suspension to each of the four prepared cultures (not to the duplicate non-inoculated controls)

2. To all cultures, including controls, add 300 μL of 1 mM DCPIP solution (final concentration 30 μM)

3. Incubate all tubes with lids slightly loose at a 45° angle at 37 °C with shaking at 180 rpm

**Tracking redox activity**

1. Every day, transfer 150 μL of cultures into eppendorf tubes

2. Centrifuge at 10,000 xg for 5 minutes to pellet plastic particles and bacterial cells

3. Take 100 mL supernatant and transfer to a cuvette, dilute 1/10 with 900 μL bacterial LCFM

4. Measure OD at 600 nm using bacterial LCFM as the reference

5. To detect redox activity compare the reduction of the blue colour, and qunatifiably the reduction in the absorbance of the DCPIP, in the presence of plastic compared to DCPIP only and the uninoculated DCPIP only control
